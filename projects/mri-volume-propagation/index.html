MEDICAL IMAGING · PROJECT 04

Interactive MRI Volume Segmentation

Prompt one MRI slice, propagate the mask through the volume, then review and export it.

DICOMNIfTISegment AnythingXMemGradio

One annotation, many slices

Manual slice-by-slice segmentation is slow. The prototype uses SAM to create a seed mask and XMem’s memory mechanism to propagate it through the volume.

  1. LoadAccept NIfTI, DICOM ZIP, or NumPy volumes and navigate axial, sagittal, or coronal views.
  2. PromptPlace foreground points on a selected slice and generate a SAM mask.
  3. PropagateUse XMem to carry the seeded object mask across neighbouring slices.
  4. ReviewInspect overlays, contours, slice coverage, and abrupt inter-slice changes.
  5. ExportSave the volume mask as NIfTI, NumPy, or a zipped PNG stack.

What is demonstrated

Product-shaped engineering

  • Modular volume loader, mask store, SAM wrapper, XMem propagator, and export layer.
  • Lazy model loading and automatic CUDA/CPU selection.
  • An interactive Gradio workflow rather than a notebook-only proof of concept.
  • An anatomy-aware cleanup path for thresholding, hole filling, continuity scoring, and component selection.

Evidence boundary

High consecutive-slice overlap shows continuity, but adjacent anatomy is naturally similar. Without expert masks, these values cannot establish segmentation accuracy or clinical utility. The interactive backward-propagation path also needs a final seed-state regression test before release.

Prototype status. Working interface, saved DICOM outputs, and consistency diagnostics are present. Expert-labelled validation and usability testing remain open.
