MEDICAL COMPUTER VISION · PROJECT 02

Surgical Tissue Localization & Deformation Tracking

A research prototype that tracks deforming tissue, estimates when those tracks become unreliable, and converts point trajectories into temporal and deformation evidence.

PyTorchCoTracker3MC DropoutTemporal analysisLocal strainGitHub ↗

The surgical problem

Tissue is elastic, instruments create occlusions, and endoscopic lighting changes as the camera moves. A point tracker can look plausible while silently drifting.

The prototype was designed around a more useful question than “did the point move?”: can the downstream system understand how much to trust that motion?

Pipeline

  1. Sample50 candidate tissue points from the initial segmentation mask.
  2. TrackRun CoTracker3 across overlapping 50-frame blocks with 16-frame overlap.
  3. Estimate uncertaintyAggregate three stochastic MC-Dropout passes into mean trajectories and positional variance.
  4. Analyse behaviourMeasure visibility, smoothness, cumulative drift, disappearance/recovery events, local motion, and neighbour-based strain.
  5. Communicate failureRender reliability halos, augmented video overlays, and a temporal-analysis dashboard.

What the evidence says

Useful signals

  • Visibility remained measurable across the preview, ending with 38 of 50 points visible.
  • MC-Dropout produced a mean positional standard deviation of 5.86 px; the 95th percentile reached 23.74 px, supporting point-level reliability cues.
  • The temporal layer recorded 246 disappearance transitions and 234 recoveries, exposing instability that a final-frame metric would hide.
  • Local strain was estimated from changes in each point’s nearest-neighbour geometry, creating a deformation signal rather than only a displacement trace.

What did not yet validate

  • The initial smoothness and drift thresholds rejected every trajectory. The filtering rule therefore needs scale-aware calibration before it can be presented as a quality gate.
  • Tool proximity and local strain showed a weak preliminary correlation (r = 0.09). The current run does not support a relationship claim.
  • The evidence covers 200 of 1,122 OP1 frames with three stochastic passes. OP2 to OP4 and ground-truth endpoint error remain future work.
Evidence boundary. Metrics above were recomputed from the saved OP1 arrays in the project archive. They describe a technical prototype, not a clinically validated navigation system.

System modules

tissue_localizer.py

Point sampling, block-wise sequence tracking, and result aggregation.

mc_dropout.py

Stochastic inference wrapper that keeps dropout active during evaluation.

temporal_analyzer.py

Smoothness, drift, survival, occlusion events, motion vectors, and local strain.

visualize_temporal.py

Diagnostic dashboard and temporal failure visualization.

annotate_cotton.py

Gradio polygon tool for selecting, undoing, closing, and saving cotton-boundary annotations.

Cutie + OP4 notebooks

Prepared full-sequence mask and boundary propagation experiments for 1,125 OP4 frames and 45 supplied masks; completed quantitative results are not yet present.
