MEDICAL MICROSCOPY · PROJECT 05

Blood Cell Detection: The Platelet Problem

Deformable DETR experiments for white cells, red cells, and small platelet detection.

Deformable DETRBCCDPyTorchCOCO mAPTiled inferenceGitHub ↗

The hidden failure

Standard full-image inference performed well on larger cells but produced zero small-object AP for platelets in one evaluation run.

Rather than hiding that result, the project treated it as the central engineering problem. The pipeline moved from standard DETR experiments to Deformable DETR, then compared full-image inference with a 2×2 overlapping tile strategy.

Reproducible workflow

  • Converted Pascal VOC annotations into COCO JSON with train, validation, and test splits.
  • Fine-tuned Deformable DETR for WBC, RBC, and Platelets.
  • Tested Albumentations, overlapping tiling, and class-aware NMS.
  • Generated COCO metrics, precision and recall curves, confusion matrices, and overlays.

The result is a trade-off

What improved

Tiling increased mAP@50 and changed platelet AP from 0.000 to 0.583 in the saved ablation report. Magnifying each quadrant made very small cells visible to the detector’s feature hierarchy.

What regressed

Large-object AP fell from 0.701 to 0.434. Tile boundaries can split large white cells, and NMS cannot always reconstruct a clean full-object detection from partial views.

Reporting caveat

The archive contains multiple checkpoints and reports: the main README records mAP@50:95 of 0.519 and mAP@50 of 0.844, while the later tiling report records the comparison above. These are retained as separate experimental runs rather than combined into one “best model” claim.

Research conclusion. Tiling is valuable when platelet recall matters, but it is not a free improvement. A production design should use multi-scale validation, tile-edge handling, and per-class operating thresholds.
